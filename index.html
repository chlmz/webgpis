Our Culture

GPIS is built on horizontal structure, mutual trust, and collaboration. Guided by social impact and constant innovation, we aim to transform research into real-world health improvements.

Our Vision

To become a leading, well-funded research group managing large-scale projects with direct social impact in public health.

Primary Project: 2019 Rio Grande Birth Cohort

A prospective, population-based cohort of 2,051 mothers and infants born in Rio Grande (2019). We study mental health, intergenerational effects, and crisis impacts such as COVID-19 and floods.

  • Wearable cameras & AI to study mother-child interactions
  • Flood impact studies on maternal and child mental health

Collaborators: Rebecca Pearson (MMU), Ana Luiza Soares (Bristol), Marc Bornstein (NICHD).
